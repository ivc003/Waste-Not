# The Issue In the United States, a third of all food is wasted. This translates to roughly a 100 billion pounds annually that could feed 150 million people. Instead, it lies in landfills, releasing millions of tons of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. # Our Solution Our proposed solution is a method to target food waste from grocery businesses by redistributing them directly to local restaurants. That will serve to connect grocery stores and restaurants across the city. By providing a medium to connect two businesses-- one with excess inventory and one with a constant need for it-- we can develop mutually beneficial relationships, redirecting what would be wasted food and recapturing it as cost reduction. For the restaurants, it's another way to support locals community and save from lower transportation fees. It's a win for both sides and an even bigger victory for the environment with the reduction of greenhouse gas emissions. ## Why does it even matter? Food waste releases roughly 170 million metric tons of carbon dioxide annually as well as methane, a gas 28x more efficient at warming than CO2. This, alongside food loss, accounts for 10% of greenhouse gas emissions which is 5x the total emissions from the aviation sector. All of these gases contribute to climate change, and the serious human and economic impacts it has on our society. Instead of feeding people, they are contributing to a negative feedback loop, changing weather patterns and creating droughts which damage our agriculture and food production. ## Why not food banks? Existing food solutions focus on reducing food waste on, and by giving it to, the consumer level. Incentivized with minor tax benefits, stores can donate extra inventory in certain states which gets distributed to people through food banks and other organizations. However, most lack the commercial-grade refrigeration or capacity to accept non-perishable goods like groceries and instead opt for canned foods and boxed items as their inventory. As a result, our foundation lies in a different niche all together by providing restaurants with fresh produce from local distributors at a fraction of the cost. ## How much is being saved? For retailers who are profiting from otherwise-trashed inventory, the benefits for restaurants may seem a little less definite. However, our price matching is intended to ensure that our partners will recieve the right produce at the right time and for the right price. Our recommender system is designed with this all in mind, accounting for factors like freshness when considering inventory discounts. As our forcasting model supports, this potentially translates to savings for consumers as well, lowering the growing cost of ingredients that's currently putting strain on our food industry. ## How is this saving the environment? This is a solution targeting climate change by providing a monetary and community incentive for businesses and people to participate. Rooted by symbiotic relationships, our solution avoids the tragedy of the commons that plagues the current framework of environmental solution. By addressing the issue with a streamlined solution that fits a previously empty niche, we can deliver results by capitalizing on uncaptured resources. Here, we can reduce food waste and greenhouse gas emissions in an area with enormous potential.
